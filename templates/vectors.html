Vectors

Definition

A vector is a quantity with both magnitude and direction. In 2D, a vector \(\vec{v}\) can be written as:

\[ \vec{v} = \begin{pmatrix} a \\ b \end{pmatrix} = a\,\hat{\imath} + b\,\hat{\jmath} \]

Magnitude

The magnitude of \(\vec{v} = \langle a, b \rangle\) is:

\[ \|\vec{v}\| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2} \]

Vector Operations

Addition:

\[ \vec{u} + \vec{v} = \langle u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2 \rangle \]

Scalar multiplication:

\[ k\,\vec{v} = \langle k\,a, k\,b \rangle \]

Dot Product

The dot product of \(\vec{u} = \langle u_1, u_2 \rangle\) and \(\vec{v} = \langle v_1, v_2 \rangle\) is:

\[ \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_1 v_1 + u_2 v_2 \]
