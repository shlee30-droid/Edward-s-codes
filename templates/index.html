01 // COMPLEX NUMBERS: i-UNIT

Algebra II introduces the **imaginary unit, $i$**, which allows us to solve quadratic equations that have no real solutions. This expands our number system into the **Complex Plane**.

THE IMAGINARY UNIT $i$

The unit is defined as the square root of negative one:

DEFINITION

$$i = \sqrt{-1}$$ $$i^2 = -1$$
[Image of the complex number plane]

THE COMPLEX NUMBER FORM

A complex number $z$ is written in the form $a + bi$, where $a$ is the **real** part and $bi$ is the **imaginary** part.

EXAMPLE: ADDITION

$$(4 + 2i) + (1 - 7i)$$ $$(4 + 1) + (2i - 7i)$$ $$5 - 5i$$

POWERS OF $i$ (THE CYCLE)

The powers of $i$ follow a repeating cycle of four values:

$i^1 = i$
$i^2 = -1$
$i^3 = -i$
$i^4 = 1$
